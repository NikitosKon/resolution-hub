## Official facts

### F1 — A restriction notice should be checked in eBay Messages

- **Claim:** eBay says that when an account is restricted or suspended, it sends an email and a copy to eBay Messages explaining why and what the seller needs to do. The UK help page specifically directs the user to check Messages for the details.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the UK-facing help page; applicability outside that registered site must be confirmed.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.co.uk help; use as a process principle, not as proof of identical wording in every market.
- **Supported use:** the triage page may tell readers to compare the email with the authenticated copy in eBay Messages before acting.

### F2 — Restrictions can affect multiple account functions and payouts

- **Claim:** Depending on the reason, eBay lists possible effects such as being unable to bid, buy, sell, create or revise listings, contact bidders, reply to messages, or update personal information. Seller payouts may also be placed on hold until the restriction or suspension is lifted.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for eBay.co.uk help; the exact effects remain case-dependent.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.co.uk help page; do not present the list as an exhaustive global rule.
- **Supported use:** a diagnostic matrix may ask which function is actually blocked rather than assuming every MC011 affects the same feature set.

### F3 — eBay says the message-specific steps control the next action

- **Claim:** eBay states that reinstatement depends on the reason for the restriction; the details and required steps are in the message sent to the account. Possible paths include waiting for a temporary suspension, making a change to listings, paying an outstanding balance, or sending additional information.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the general process; LOW for predicting any individual outcome.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.co.uk help; market-specific message instructions take precedence.
- **Supported use:** the guide should distinguish “what eBay’s message asks” from generic advice and should not promise a result.

### F4 — eBay documents that identity or account verification can be required for selling

- **Claim:** eBay’s US seller-registration help says eBay may verify identity, linked payout details, and business details; if details cannot be verified, eBay may ask for more information or display a banner with the required action. Payouts may be unavailable until verification is complete.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH within the seller-registration context; it is not an MC011-specific universal rule.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.com / US-facing seller registration. Do not transplant its document list or file limits to another market.
- **Supported use:** explain why an MC011 message can contain verification language without asserting that every MC011 is a verification request.

### F5 — In the US seller-registration context, uploaded files have documented technical requirements

- **Claim:** The US seller-registration page says uploaded documents should be current, match the account information, be clearly readable and show all four corners; it lists JPEG/JPG/PDF/PNG formats, a 4 MB maximum for photo IDs and 10 MB for other documents.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the cited US seller-registration workflow; LOW for applying these limits to an MC011 message or another market.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.com seller-registration page only.
- **Supported use:** only as a clearly labeled example of why the exact notice and market must be checked; do not publish it as a universal MC011 upload rule.

### F6 — eBay Japan explicitly names MC011 and separates ordinary restriction handling from message-specific exceptions

- **Claim:** eBay Japan’s account guidance, updated 2026-06-10, names the subject `[MC011 We restricted your account]`, describes a restriction as potentially blocking new listings/editing and temporarily holding sales proceeds, and says that the notice may request additional documents or a specific action such as tracking-number submission. The page instructs sellers to follow the content of the email in those exceptions.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — official eBay Japan guidance.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for eBay Japan; LOW for global generalization.
- **Market applicability:** eBay Japan / Japanese cross-border seller guidance. It must not be represented as the universal document set for US, UK, Ukraine, EU, or another market.
- **Supported use:** this is the strongest official MC011-specific source found, but only for a market-bounded example. The page may explain why a triage decision tree asks whether the message lists a special action.

### F7 — eBay Japan publishes a market-specific three-document example and explicit exception language

- **Claim:** eBay Japan lists photo ID, address verification, and inventory evidence for the standard MC011 example, then says that if the email requests additional documents or a specific action, the seller should follow that email instead. Its attachment conditions are GIF/JPG/PNG/PDF, up to 2 MB per file and 20 MB total, with English preferred but Japanese accepted.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — official eBay Japan guidance.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the Japanese page; LOW outside its stated market/context.
- **Market applicability:** eBay Japan only. No global MC011 checklist is established by this source.
- **Supported use:** a warning box can say “do not copy this list to another market”; the page must send readers back to their own authenticated notice.

### F8 — eBay provides a separate appeal process and says eligibility is case-by-case

- **Claim:** eBay UK’s appeal page says a user can appeal an action believed to be unjustified, but eBay will explain case-by-case in its emails when an appeal can be filed. The page lists examples such as account suspension or inability to verify a commercial seller; it also routes payment-service, blocked-payout, and account-verification issues to the relevant help or support path rather than treating every issue as the same appeal type.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay UK regulatory/help page.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the page’s UK process description; LOW for inferring eligibility for a particular reader.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.co.uk regulatory/help context; local rights and process may differ.
- **Supported use:** keep “appeal available” separate from “appeal guaranteed” and make the reader check the notice for eligibility.

### F9 — eBay warns that important contacts should also appear in eBay Messages

- **Claim:** eBay’s security help says the easiest way to identify an authentic account email is to check eBay Messages; important messages are copied there. eBay says it will not ask for passwords or card details, will not include unsolicited attachments, and will not use threatening language in legitimate contacts.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay security help.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the security guidance.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.com-facing security page; use as a safety check, not as evidence of the account restriction itself.
- **Supported use:** the first triage step should include checking the authenticated Messages copy and avoiding password/card disclosure.

### F10 — eBay prohibits creating additional accounts to circumvent restrictions

- **Claim:** eBay’s User Agreement policy page says members must not create additional accounts to circumvent selling restrictions, buying limits, suspension, or other policy consequences. It also prohibits false or incomplete information used to circumvent policies.
- **Source level:** Level 1 — current official eBay policy.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for the policy statement.
- **Market applicability:** eBay.com policy page; confirm local agreement if the article later makes a jurisdiction-specific legal claim.
- **Supported use:** include a safety warning against opening or using another account as a workaround; do not give evasion advice.
