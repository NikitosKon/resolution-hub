# Research: paypal-held-funds-status-checklist

## Research context

- **Content-plan row:** `resource-paypal-held-funds-status`
- **Platform:** PayPal
- **Planned language:** Russian (`ru`); any later English or Ukrainian edition must preserve the same source boundaries and be checked separately.
- **Primary topic:** how to read a PayPal held-funds, reserve, or unavailable-balance notice and identify what to verify first.
- **Planned intent:** diagnostic / consideration. The page is a status-reading checklist, not a promise that funds will be released.
- **Proposed resource URL:** `/ru/paypal/held-funds-status-checklist/` (not published and not yet approved as a route).
- **Research date / access date:** 2026-07-23.
- **Market scope:** The target audience is Russian-speaking, but the PayPal account's registered country and governing agreement determine applicable terms, notices, features, and timelines. Current official pages reviewed here cover the United States, United Kingdom, Ukraine/EU-facing materials, and PayPal Pte. Ltd. contexts. They must not be collapsed into one universal rule.
- **Research language:** English official PayPal legal/help pages; the eventual guide may use Russian wording but must retain the market qualification.
- **Device/context:** logged-out public web retrieval on desktop. No authenticated account, Resolution Centre case, notice, balance screen, or transaction details were available.
- **Query scope:** current official definitions and diagnostic signals for payment holds, account limitations, reserves, pending/unavailable labels, account notifications, Resolution Centre instructions, and market-specific differences.
- **Declared knowledge inputs:** `knowledge/platform-facts/README.md` contains no approved PayPal fact record specific to this new slug. No matching confirmed owner experience was supplied.
- **Scope boundary:** This artifact records evidence only. It does not draft an article, choose a final outline, provide a CTA, recommend a universal document list, or diagnose a reader's account.

The central research finding is that a visible label such as “on hold,” “pending,” or “unavailable” does not identify one mechanism. PayPal's official material distinguishes transaction-level holds, account limitations, reserves, payment review, and marketplace-directed holds. The account notice and governing market must be checked before any next step or timing is discussed.

### Source-bound distinctions that must survive editing

1. **Hold, limitation, reserve, review, and pending are not interchangeable.** The guide must first ask the reader to preserve the exact account/transaction wording and identify whether sending, receiving, or withdrawing is also affected.
2. **A displayed status is not a cause.** A list of possible factors from an official help page must not be rewritten as “PayPal held your funds because of X.”
3. **A market-specific timeframe is not a universal countdown.** The U.S. agreement's “up to 21 days” risk-hold language and the U.K. help page's “aim to review within three days after required steps” must not be merged with 180-day account-closure wording or applied to Ukraine without the controlling agreement.
4. **Reserve terms and a release date come from the account notice/balance view.** Public sources do not justify predicting when a particular balance will become withdrawable.
5. **A marketplace-directed hold is not automatically a PayPal risk decision.** If the notice names a marketplace or third-party service, the reader may need that party's support route as well.
6. **A release control is conditional.** “Know your options,” tracking, identity confirmation, or any other control can be mentioned only when the account itself shows that option and the relevant official source supports it.

## Observed patterns

No Level 3 policy pattern is established. This research used current official PayPal sources to define terms and account-specific checks. No forum, Reddit, YouTube, or social-media report is treated as evidence of PayPal policy, a release rate, a typical cause, or a typical timeline.

The following recurring signals appear across the official pages, but remain **Level 1 source observations**, not universal rules:

- PayPal repeatedly directs the user to the exact account notice, dashboard alert, Resolution Center, or balance details for the applicable next step (S1, S2, S3, S5, S6, S8).
