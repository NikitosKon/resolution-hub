# Intent Analysis

**Primary diagnostic intent:** the reader already sees a status or notice and needs to classify what is affected before acting. The safest workflow is: preserve exact wording/date; identify payment, withdrawal, balance/reserve, or account limitation; check the account surface named by PayPal; record whether sending, receiving, or withdrawing is affected; treat cause, timing, documents, and release options as conditional.

**Secondary intents:** terminology; risk triage; marketplace/third-party hold; safe evidence capture; whether a visible review/release date exists.

**Boundary:** this resource must not become the existing 180-day account-closure guide, a universal document checklist, or a promise of release/appeal success.

# Common H2

Observed headings and recurring structures (paraphrased, not a template to copy):

- Why is my payment held or unavailable?
- What does my payment status mean?
- What should I do if a withdrawal is under review?
- Account reserves / types of reserves
- How to view details or available options
- Additional help resources, Resolution Center, and contact support
- Legal sections defining holds, limitations, reserves, and notices

The common pattern is short and action-oriented. Resolution Hub can retain that directness while adding a classification matrix and explicit market caveats.

# Missing Topics

1. Status-to-scope matrix preventing “pending” and “held” from being treated as identical.
2. First screen for payment, withdrawal, reserve/balance, or account limitation.
3. Market and account-type field before any timing/document interpretation.
4. Checklist to copy exact wording, date, affected function, and transaction context.
5. Difference between a possible official example and a confirmed cause.
6. Conditional branch for marketplace/third-party-directed holds.
7. Privacy-safe redaction reminder.
8. What public pages cannot determine, including confidential risk criteria and release outcome.
9. Separation of withdrawal review from payment hold and account limitation.
10. A bounded handoff to `/ru/paypal/funds-held-180-days/` only for permanent-limitation/180-day questions.
11. Checked date and source-market register.
12. Questions for support that do not imply disclosure of confidential risk logic.

# Opportunities

- Own the diagnostic angle with a status-first checklist.
- Use a four-column table: “what you see”, “what it may refer to”, “what to check”, “what cannot be inferred”.
- Add a restrained process diagram: exact notice → affected object → official account route → requested action → recorded response.
- Add a “do not assume” callout: a label alone does not establish cause, deadline, or release.
- Ask readers to confirm registered market without pretending to localize a global rule.
- Provide a safe evidence worksheet (date, exact phrase, redacted transaction suffix, affected function, account type, instruction shown).
- Link to the 180-day guide only for permanent limitation/closure intent.
- Include source links with roles (agreement vs help article), access date, and market applicability.

# Recommended Angle

**PayPal удержание средств: как разобрать точный статус и понять, что проверить первым**

Position the page as a Russian-language, market-aware triage sheet:

1. Start with exact status and affected object, not a guessed cause.
2. Use a four-way matrix: transaction hold, withdrawal review, reserve/unavailable balance, account limitation.
3. Point to the official account surface conditionally.
4. Separate market-specific examples from universal diagnostic fields.
5. Show evidence to record and sensitive data to redact.
6. State what cannot be predicted: individual cause, release date, or appeal outcome.
7. Link to the 180-day guide only for permanent-limitation/closure questions.

This angle adds practical classification without copying the short help pages or overstating policy.
