# Page promise

- **Primary intent:** Help a reader classify an eBay MC011 or related restriction message from the authenticated account context before taking any consequential action.
- **Reader outcome:** The reader can verify the in-account copy, record the registered eBay market and seller context, identify the affected function and the exact requested task, and choose a safe next check or a deliberate pause.
- **Proposed H1:** eBay MC011: как разобрать сообщение и понять, что проверить первым
- **Language / market boundary:** Russian resource. Language does not establish the registered eBay market; all market-specific examples must retain their source label.
- **Promise boundary:** Diagnostic triage only. This is not a universal document list, evidence-preparation workflow, payout-remediation guide, appeal strategy, recovery promise, or explanation of why a particular account was restricted.

# Adaptive-structure rationale

- **Issue shape:** “MC011” is a code or message reference, not enough information to identify the reader's cause, required evidence, deadline, route, affected function, or result. The page therefore starts with authentication and extraction rather than a definition or generic causes list.
- **Reader state:** The reader may be about to send sensitive records, follow a spoofed email, open another account, or assume that a visible payout/listing effect proves the cause. The highest-risk checks appear before any contextual examples.
- **Evidence shape:** Current first-party evidence supports checking eBay Messages, following message-specific steps, and treating function/payout effects as conditional. The MC011-specific example found is eBay Japan only; a Russian page must not convert it into a global checklist.
- **Decision shape:** A single request-to-route matrix is more useful than a sequence of recovery steps. It classifies `general restriction`, `concrete evidence request`, `payout branch`, `appeal question`, or `other named action`, then hands off to the narrowest live guide.
- **Differentiation:** `mc011-restriction` owns broad meaning, possible effects, authenticity context, and first checks. `mc011-documents-requested` owns evidence extraction and preparation after an authenticated request. This page owns the intermediate triage decision and stops at the handoff.
- **Risk shape:** Missing or mismatched records are treated as unresolved; no substitute, invented cause, timing, outcome, or workaround is supplied. The anti-fabrication and anti-circumvention boundary is placed at the classification gate where it can prevent harm.
- **Conversion shape:** The reader receives a complete self-check first. Individual review is an optional final transition for redacted facts that remain unclassified, not a substitute for eBay's authenticated route.
- **Media shape:** One original decision map and one notice-record worksheet can clarify the flow. No eBay interface or account screenshot is recreated; any future visual must be labelled as a Resolution Hub illustration.

# Narrative order

1. State that MC011 alone is not a diagnosis and name the four facts that control the next check: authenticated notice, registered market, seller/account context, and affected function.
2. Verify that the same request appears in the signed-in eBay account and tell the reader to stop before opening an unverified link or sending information.
3. Ask the reader to record the exact message, date, named action, deadline if shown, submission route if shown, and current visible effect without interpreting it.
4. Use the request-to-route matrix to classify the message as general restriction, concrete evidence request, payout branch, appeal question, or another named task.
5. Branch only on what the notice actually names: show the relevant official source/route and keep all other categories marked `NOT_IN_NOTICE`.
6. Put market and seller-type labels beside every contextual example; explicitly separate eBay Japan MC011 guidance from US/UK verification guidance.
7. Place the genuine-record and anti-circumvention gate before any suggestion to reply or upload; missing, mismatched, or unknown records lead to `PAUSE`, never an improvised substitute.
8. Keep format, size, language, deadline, channel, and receipt confirmation as notice-derived fields; do not fill them with cross-market defaults.
9. End the self-check with three states: `CLASSIFIED_AND_ROUTE_VISIBLE`, `NEEDS_ACCOUNT_CLARIFICATION`, or `WRONG_GUIDE / HANDOFF`.
10. Only after the gate, qualify payouts and appeal as conditional adjacent branches, state what timing/outcome cannot be known, link to the document guide when appropriate, and offer optional redacted individual review.
